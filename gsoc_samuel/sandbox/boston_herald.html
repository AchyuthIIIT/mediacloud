Barney Frank, John Tierney lead global treks

Pols jet past junket pinch

By Dave Wedge
Friday, June 19, 2009 - Updated 6h ago

E-mail   Print   (11) Comments   Text size  Share  

The congressional crackdown on special interest junkets hasn’t grounded Bay State Democrats who continue to rack up frequent flier miles with trips around the globe for them and their staff members.

U.S. Rep. John Tierney (D-Salem) had his passport stamped the most, taking a $16,000 trip to Jordan for a conference on Islam in February and a $9,000 jaunt to Paris in August 2008. The tabs for both were paid by the nonpartisan think tank Aspen Institute.

U.S. Rep. Barney Frank (D-Newton), meanwhile, took $6,500 in free trips in 2008, traveling to conferences in New York and Chicago and to Hollywood to appear as a guest on HBO’s “Real Time with Bill Maher.” The show picked up his $1,998 tab.

And staffers to the Bay State delegation jetted off to exotic locales, including Cuba and Turkey and a host of other international spots from Germany to England.

Congress cracked down on special interest trips in 2007, following the corruption scandal involving lobbyist Jack Abramoff. A new law bars lawmakers from traveling with lobbyists and requires paid congressional trips to be approved by ethics officials.

But Frank defended his trips, saying, “None of them are vacations. Every one of those relates to the job I’m supposed to do. They were all things I did because they advanced the kinds of causes I’m in politics to advance.”

“Shows like Bill Maher are very useful,” he said. “It’s not just entertainment. I think it’s a very valuable public resource. Plus, there’s nothing they want from me, so there’s no quid pro quo.”

Tierney spokeswoman Catherine Ribeiro said he “felt these trips were beneficial as the topics were all related to the congressman’s committee assignments and areas under their jurisdiction.”

The Aspen Institute, one of a handful of groups that federal ethics officials have cleared to pay for congressional travel, also spent $2,100 to send Tierney to an education seminar in Charleston, S.C., in February 2008, according to records compiled by the government watchdog Legistorm.

Tierney also took $19,000 worth of Aspen Institute-funded trips in 2007, shoving off to Slovenia, Costa Rica, Liberia and Puerto Rico, records show.

In June 2008, Frank took a $572 trip to New York for a forum on teen suicide in the gay community, which was paid for by the gay nonprofit Hetrick-Martin Institute. In December, he was back in New York to chair a National Gay and Lesbian Chamber of Commerce event. The organization paid his $1,427 travel bill, records show.

“I do a lot of gay and lesbian stuff because there are not a lot of us,” Frank said. “I think it’s important to show we’re part of the mainstream.”

He also attended a housing conference in Chicago at a cost of $1,069, which was paid for by the nonprofit Congress for the New Urbanism, and spoke at a finance seminar at Princeton University. The college paid his $1,291 tab.

The only other Bay State lawmaker to take a junket in 2008 was Sen. John F. Kerry, who traveled to Spain in April for an energy summit. His $4,374 bill was paid by the Aspen Institute, records show.
